# Proof that $\sqrt{2}$ is irrational

<LogicStep client:load kind="assumption" delay={0.1}>
Assume $\sqrt{2} = \frac{a}{b}$ with integers $a, b$ in lowest terms.
</LogicStep>

<LogicStep client:load kind="implication" delay={0.3}>
Squaring gives $2 = \frac{a^2}{b^2} \Rightarrow a^2 = 2b^2$
</LogicStep>

<LogicStep client:load kind="implication" delay={0.5}>
So $a^2$ is even, hence $a$ is even $\Rightarrow a = 2k$.
</LogicStep>

<LogicStep client:load kind="implication" delay={0.7}>
Then $a^2 = 4k^2 = 2b^2 \Rightarrow b^2 = 2k^2 \Rightarrow b$ is even.
</LogicStep>

<LogicStep client:load kind="contradiction" delay={0.9}>
But both $a$ and $b$ are even â€” contradicting the lowest terms assumption!
</LogicStep>

<LogicStep client:load kind="neutral" delay={1.1}>
$\therefore$ $\sqrt{2}$ is irrational.
</LogicStep>
